# 9-Quine

The 9-quine is a simpler quine graph with only 9 nodes that still exhibits self-replication properties. It's used in experiments on quine behavior and senescence.

## What is 9-Quine?

The **9-quine** is a self-replicating graph that:
- Has only **9 nodes** (simpler than Ouroboros)
- Still exhibits **replication** behavior
- Used in **senescence studies**
- Demonstrates that **small graphs can be quines**

## Structure

### 9 Nodes

The graph consists of:
- **9 nodes total**: Very small compared to other quines
- **Simple structure**: Simpler than Ouroboros
- **Still functional**: Can still replicate

### Graph Properties

- **Minimal quine**: One of the smallest quines known
- **Replicable**: Can produce copies of itself
- **Stable**: Shows stable replication behavior

## Replication Mechanism

### Same as Other Quines

The 9-quine replicates through:
1. **Pattern matching**: Finding non-conflicting rewrite patterns
2. **Parallel application**: Applying all matches simultaneously
3. **Isomorphism**: Result is isomorphic to original
4. **Replication**: Produces copy of itself

### Simplicity

Despite being small:
- **Still works**: Replication mechanism still works
- **Same principles**: Uses same principles as larger quines
- **Demonstrates**: Shows quines don't need to be complex
